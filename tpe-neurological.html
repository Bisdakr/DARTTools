TPE for Neurological Disease
Detailed reference for plasma exchange in the most common autoimmune neurological indications: Guillain-Barré syndrome, myasthenia gravis crisis, CIDP, anti-NMDA receptor encephalitis, NMOSD, ANCA-associated vasculitis with CNS, autoimmune encephalitis spectrum, and acute disseminated encephalomyelitis.

TL;DR — Antibody Removal in Neuroautoimmunity
Plasma exchange removes pathogenic IgG antibodies, immune complexes, complement, and inflammatory cytokines from circulation. In neurological autoimmune disease, this provides rapid disease modification when steroids and IVIG alone are insufficient. The Spectra Optia TPE procedure typically uses 1.0–1.5 plasma volume exchange per session, 5–7 sessions on alternating days, with albumin or albumin/FFP replacement depending on indication. Time-to-treatment matters: earlier is better for GBS, MG crisis, and acute NMOSD attacks.
01
Overview of TPE in Neurological Disease

TPE's role in neurology is built on the principle of removing circulating autoantibodies that drive neuromuscular junction disease (MG), demyelination (GBS, CIDP, NMOSD, MS relapse), or synaptic / neuronal autoimmunity (NMDAR, LGI1, GABA-B encephalitis). It is a bridge therapy: while immunomodulation begins, TPE provides rapid symptomatic improvement.

ParameterStandard TPE Approach
Plasma volume exchanged1.0–1.5 PV per session (removes ~63% of IgG with 1 PV; ~78% with 1.5 PV)
FrequencyAlternate-day typically; daily acceptable in critical illness
Number of sessions5–7 sessions for most neurological indications
Replacement fluid5% albumin standard; FFP added if coagulopathy, bleeding risk, or specific indications (e.g., TTP, CAPS)
Vascular accessPeripheral if adequate veins; central catheter for ICU patients or repeated courses
AnticoagulationCitrate (ACD-A) at 15:1 inlet ratio; heparin alternative if citrate-intolerant
Concurrent immunomodulationSteroids and/or immunosuppression usually started concurrently; IVIG and TPE generally not given simultaneously (TPE removes IVIG)
MonitoringDisease-specific clinical scores, complete blood count, fibrinogen, ionized calcium, electrolytes between sessions
🔑
TPE vs. IVIGFor most neurological indications, TPE and IVIG are roughly equivalent in efficacy. TPE acts faster (improvement often within 24–48 hours) but requires vascular access and equipment. IVIG is administered as standard infusion but takes longer to act and is contraindicated in IgA deficiency. Do not give simultaneously; if sequencing, give IVIG after TPE so it is not removed.
02
Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS)
Guillain-Barré Syndrome ASFA Cat I
Acute inflammatory demyelinating polyradiculoneuropathy (AIDP) and variants (AMAN, Miller-Fisher). Autoantibody-mediated; often post-infectious (Campylobacter, CMV, EBV, COVID-19, Zika). Ascending weakness, areflexia, dysautonomia, respiratory failure in 25–30%.
Treatment Course
5 sessions over 10–14 days
Plasma Volume
1.0–1.5 PV per session
Replacement
5% albumin
Window
Within 2–4 weeks of onset; sooner = better
🧪
Outcomes DataCochrane meta-analyses demonstrate TPE significantly hastens recovery in GBS — reduced time on ventilator, time to walk independently, time to recover from disability. Effect equivalent to IVIG. Either is standard of care; institution / availability often drives choice.
  • Severity criteria: Patients unable to walk unaided, rapidly progressive disease, respiratory compromise, autonomic instability.
  • Do not combine with IVIG — Adds cost and risk without added benefit.
  • Watch for dysautonomia — Hypotension during TPE; arrhythmias. Continuous telemetry recommended.
  • Treatment-related fluctuation — A subset (~5–10%) deteriorate after initial improvement; may need re-treatment.
03
Myasthenia Gravis (Crisis & Pre-Thymectomy)
MG Crisis & Pre-Thymectomy ASFA Cat I
Autoantibodies to acetylcholine receptor (AChR), MuSK, or LRP4. Crisis = respiratory failure requiring ventilation or impending. Pre-thymectomy = elective stabilization before thymus surgery.
Treatment Course
5–7 sessions over 7–14 days
Plasma Volume
1.0–1.5 PV per session
Replacement
5% albumin
Onset of Effect
Often within 24–48 hr
  • Crisis recognition: NIF < −20 cm H₂O, FVC < 15 mL/kg, bulbar dysfunction, single-breath count < 20.
  • Monitor for cholinergic exacerbation — Pyridostigmine often held during TPE.
  • Coordinate with immunology / neurology — Most patients continue or start immunosuppression (steroids, mycophenolate, eculizumab, rituximab).
  • Eculizumab — For refractory AChR-positive generalized MG; coordinate vaccination (meningococcal) before initiation.
  • MuSK-MG — Often more responsive to TPE / rituximab than IVIG.
04
Chronic Inflammatory Demyelinating Polyradiculoneuropathy (CIDP)
CIDP ASFA Cat I
Chronic or relapsing demyelinating polyneuropathy; progression > 8 weeks (distinguishes from GBS). Stepwise or progressive weakness; symmetric or asymmetric. IVIG, steroids, TPE all first-line.
Treatment Course
5–6 sessions over 2–3 weeks; maintenance every 4–8 weeks
Plasma Volume
1.0–1.5 PV per session
Replacement
5% albumin
Long-Term
Maintenance courses if disease relapses
  • Initial therapy: IVIG most common first-line; TPE alternative.
  • Maintenance: Some patients require ongoing periodic TPE every 4–8 weeks to maintain remission.
  • Combination: TPE + steroids may have additive benefit in refractory cases.
  • Variants: MMN (multifocal motor neuropathy) — IVIG preferred over TPE (TPE may worsen).
05
Anti-NMDA Receptor Encephalitis
Anti-NMDAR Encephalitis ASFA Cat I
Autoimmune encephalitis driven by IgG antibodies to the GluN1 subunit of the NMDA receptor. Often paraneoplastic (ovarian teratoma in young women); also post-infectious (HSV). Psychiatric symptoms → seizures → dyskinesias → autonomic instability → coma.
Treatment Course
5–7 sessions over 10–14 days; may extend
Plasma Volume
1.0–1.5 PV per session
Replacement
5% albumin
Concurrent
Steroids + tumor removal; rituximab / cyclophosphamide second-line
  • Recovery is slow — Months to years; TPE provides initial improvement.
  • Tumor screening — Pelvic ultrasound / MRI for teratoma; testicular ultrasound in males; whole-body PET if no source found.
  • ICU coordination — Patients often require prolonged ICU care for dyskinesias, autonomic crisis, status epilepticus.
  • Relapse risk — 12–25% over years; long-term immunosuppression often required.
06
Neuromyelitis Optica Spectrum Disorder (NMOSD)
NMOSD Acute Attack ASFA Cat II
Autoimmune demyelinating disease with autoantibodies to aquaporin-4 (AQP4) or MOG. Severe optic neuritis, transverse myelitis. TPE used for severe attacks not responding to high-dose steroids.
Treatment Course
5–7 sessions over 7–14 days
Plasma Volume
1.0–1.5 PV per session
Replacement
5% albumin
Window
Within 5–7 days of attack onset for best outcome
  • First-line: IV methylprednisolone 1 g daily × 5 days.
  • TPE added for severe attacks (severe vision loss, profound paresis) or no improvement after 3–5 days of steroids.
  • Maintenance: Long-term immunosuppression to prevent relapse — rituximab, eculizumab, satralizumab, inebilizumab now FDA-approved.
  • Demyelinating MS relapse: Same TPE approach for steroid-refractory severe MS attacks (ASFA Cat II).
07
ANCA-Associated Vasculitis with CNS Involvement
ANCA-Associated Vasculitis (with Severe Disease) ASFA Cat I (DAH/Cr>5.7)
Granulomatosis with polyangiitis (GPA), microscopic polyangiitis (MPA), eosinophilic GPA. TPE specifically for diffuse alveolar hemorrhage and severe renal involvement (Cr > 5.7 mg/dL or RPGN); CNS involvement less clearly studied but TPE used in select severe cases.
Treatment Course
7 sessions over 14 days
Plasma Volume
~60 mL/kg per session
Replacement
5% albumin; FFP if active bleeding
Concurrent
Rituximab or cyclophosphamide + steroids
  • PEXIVAS trial updated practice: TPE no longer routine for all severe ANCA; reserved for DAH and severe renal disease per current guidance.
  • CNS vasculitis: Limited data; TPE considered in life-threatening or steroid-refractory cases.
08
Other Autoimmune Encephalitis Subtypes

The expanding spectrum of autoantibody-mediated encephalitis (LGI1, CASPR2, GABA-B, AMPA, GAD-65, GFAP) generally responds to immunomodulation. TPE is used when steroids and IVIG are insufficient, or as first-line in severe disease.

LGI1 Encephalitis
Faciobrachial dystonic seizures; subacute cognitive decline. First-line: high-dose steroids; TPE if refractory.
CASPR2 Encephalitis
Morvan syndrome (encephalopathy + peripheral nerve hyperexcitability); TPE / IVIG / steroids + tumor search.
GABA-B Receptor Encephalitis
Severe seizures, status epilepticus; often paraneoplastic (small cell lung cancer). TPE / IVIG / steroids + tumor treatment.
Acute Disseminated Encephalomyelitis (ADEM) ASFA Cat II
Post-infectious or post-vaccine demyelinating encephalomyelitis. Steroids first-line; TPE for refractory severe cases.
PANDAS / PANS
Pediatric autoimmune neuropsychiatric disorders; TPE Category III. Coordinate with pediatric neurology / immunology.
Stiff-Person Syndrome
GAD-65 antibodies (or amphiphysin paraneoplastic); IVIG first-line; TPE in refractory cases.
