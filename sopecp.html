ECP Standards of Practice
A USA-aligned standard for performing extracorporeal photopheresis using the Therakos CELLEX integrated apheresis-photoactivation system. Aligned with FDA-approved indication (palliative CTCL), ASFA 2023 Special Issue (Category I for cGVHD and CTCL), and current device labeling.

Standard of Practice — Overview
Extracorporeal Photopheresis (ECP) is an immunomodulatory therapy in which a buffy-coat enriched fraction of the patient's blood is collected, mixed with the photosensitizer methoxsalen (UVADEX) dosed directly into the photoactivation bag, irradiated with UVA light inside the CELLEX system, and returned to the patient. ECP induces apoptosis in the treated lymphocytes, generating downstream regulatory T-cell expansion. This SOP describes the procedure using the Therakos CELLEX integrated system, with verification of the manufacturer's "13 zeros" parameter set, RBTV institutional standard 100 mL, and 24-hour UV precaution patient education.

01
Purpose & Scope

Purpose: To define the standardized procedure for extracorporeal photopheresis in adult and pediatric patients (typically ≥ 10 kg with prime adjustment) using the Therakos CELLEX system for FDA-approved palliative cutaneous T-cell lymphoma (CTCL) and ASFA-recommended graft-vs-host disease, solid organ transplant rejection, and selected autoimmune indications.

Scope: Applies to apheresis registered nurses, apheresis physicians, transplant / hematology consultants, pharmacy.

Indications recognized: CTCL palliative (FDA-approved); chronic GVHD skin (ASFA Category I); chronic GVHD bronchiolitis obliterans / lung (ASFA Category II); acute GVHD steroid-refractory; cardiac transplant cellular rejection prevention/treatment; lung transplant CLAD/BOS.

🔑
ECP is Long-Term TherapyMost ECP courses extend 6–12 months at 2 sessions/week (typically consecutive days), then taper. Patients enter long-term vascular access planning early. Coordinate with transplant / hematology service for the planned duration and taper.
02
Roles & Responsibilities

Apheresis / Cellular Therapy Physician

Reviews indication, prescribes ECP course (frequency, duration, taper); reviews pre-procedure labs including Hct ≤ 48 hr; available for emergent consultation.

Transplant / Hematology Consultant

Coordinates concurrent immunosuppression; reviews GVHD or rejection response; assesses for DVT/PE risk in GVHD patients (UVADEX label warning).

Apheresis Primary RN

Performs verification including Hct within 48 hr and lamp life check; operates the CELLEX system; performs UVADEX dosing per ECV × 0.017 calculation; monitors patient; provides UV precaution education.

Pharmacy

Supplies UVADEX 200 mcg/10 mL vial (single use); heparin 10,000 U for the procedure; reviews drug interactions.

03
Pre-Procedure Verification
  1. Two-identifier patient ID.
  2. Informed consent. ECP consent + apheresis procedure consent (typically institutional standing consent for chronic patients with annual renewal).
  3. Hematocrit verification. Per Therakos labeling, Hct must be measured within 48 hours of procedure. Documented numeric value referenced for ECV calculation.
  4. ECV % TBV check. Calculate ECV (240 mL CELLEX standard) as percentage of patient TBV. If > 15% for adult or > 10% for patient < 30 kg → consider blood prime. Raising patient Hct may bring ECV percentage under threshold (see ECV by Hct reference table).
  5. Lamp life check. Verify lamp hours remaining > 10 hours. If < 10 hours, replace lamp set before procedure. Lamp set = 18 bulbs, 150 hour total life.
  6. Vascular access verification. Peripheral IV often used; central catheter for chronic patients with poor peripheral access. Verify patency.
  7. Baseline vital signs and weight.
  8. GVHD patient — DVT/PE risk assessment. Per UVADEX label, increased DVT/PE rate reported. Padua/Caprini risk score; mechanical / pharmacologic prophylaxis per institutional protocol.
  9. Patient education preparation. Have UV precaution handout and verbal teaching ready.
04
Machine Setup & Prime
  1. Establish Ready State. Power on CELLEX. Verify interface displays "13 zeros" baseline state.
  2. Insert photoactivation module. Remove kit, hang bags, insert pump tubing organizer.
  3. Install drive tube / centrifuge bowl. Turn clockwise to secure.
  4. Load pump tubing segments from the bottom up. Verify orientation.
  5. Smart Card. Reads kit lot/expiration; verify before proceeding.
  6. Spike fluids. NS (WHITE spike), Heparin 10,000 U in 500 mL NS = 20 U/mL (ORANGE spike).
  7. Cassette install order: C-R-Y. Clear, Red, Yellow. Then bowl.
  8. Air detectors, hematocrit cuvette, pressure domes. Verify each click into place. Pressure pods LAST — spread tabs firmly, verify membrane intact (no green protective caps remaining).
  9. Hang patient lines on IV pole. Close Collect Line (RED), clean injection port.
  10. Run prime & functional test. Approximately 7 minutes automated. Address any failures.
05
Programming — Verify "13 Zeros"

Per institutional standard, verify the following programming values before initiating:

  • Return Bag Threshold (RBTV) = 100 mL (institutional standard; do not exceed)
  • Max speed collect = 50 mL/min
  • Max speed return = 50 mL/min
  • Collect Pressure Limit = 200 mmHg
  • Return Pressure Limit = 200 mmHg
  • Total blood processed = 1,500 mL max
  • Bowl Optic Threshold default = 140 mL (raise/lower by 10 if interface alarms — see Alarm #45 procedure)
06
Initiation
  1. Final pre-connection checks. ID, Hct, lamp life, programming verified.
  2. Sterile access connection. Connect Blue (return) and Red (collect) lines.
  3. Press "Prime Access". Select Single or Double needle to match access setup.
  4. Heparin bolus per institutional protocol. Common: 5,000 U bolus IV, then heparin pump via CELLEX program.
  5. Start slow: Collect 25 mL/min, Return 10 mL/min initially.
07
Phase Sequence & UVADEX Injection

The CELLEX runs a defined sequence: COLLECT → BUFFY COAT → UVA → PAUSE → ELUTRIATE → EMPTY BOWL → RINSE.

  1. COLLECT Phase 1: Purging Air.
  2. COLLECT Phase 2: Drawing / Recirculating. When you hear BEEP, OR NET balance ≤ −100, OR blood processed > 300 mL → increase: Collect 30–40, Return 35–45.
  3. BUFFY COAT phase. Open Graph → watch S-wave (Hct rises) → look for "END BUFFY" prompt.
  4. Calculate UVADEX dose at END BUFFY. The CELLEX displays the Treatment Volume (actual volume in the photoactivation bag) at the UVA prompt. UVADEX (mL) = Treatment Volume × 0.017. UVADEX concentration is 200 mcg/mL; single-use 10 mL vial; discard remainder.
  5. Inject UVADEX into the photoactivation bag per CELLEX prompt before UVA cycle starts. Verify and confirm by two-RN check at the medication injection.
  6. UVA cycle. Photoactivation runs to MINUTES REMAINING = 00:00. Do not interrupt — interrupting before completion means the calculated UVA dose is not delivered.
  7. PAUSE → ELUTRIATE → EMPTY BOWL → RINSE. Per machine prompts.
⚠
PHOTOACTIVATE Must CompleteIf the photoactivation cycle is interrupted before MINUTES REMAINING reaches 00:00, the calculated UVA dose is not delivered and the treatment effect is compromised. The lamp assembly cannot be changed mid-cycle without invalidating the dose calculation.

09
Post-Procedure & UV Precaution Education
  1. Disconnect after final RINSE phase. Standard heparin patient: 10 minutes pressure on access; longer for central catheter.
  2. Final vital signs.
  3. UV PRECAUTION × 24 HOURS — patient education. Verbal teaching plus printed handout: UV-blocking sunglasses outdoors and near windows; long sleeves, hat, pants outdoors; avoid direct sunlight and tanning beds; sunscreen SPF ≥ 30 on any exposed skin; indoors with curtains drawn ideal for first few hours.
  4. Schedule next session. Typically 2 consecutive days, then 1–2 week interval per indication. Verify patient is committed to the schedule.
  5. GVHD patient — verify DVT/PE prophylaxis status. Mechanical + pharmacologic per institutional protocol.
  6. Patient education on access care. If chronic catheter, daily exit-site inspection, sterile technique at every connection.

11
Documentation & Quality Indicators
  • Hct ≤ 48 hr documented. Numeric value with collection date.
  • Lamp life check documented. Hours remaining at procedure start.
  • UVADEX dose documented. Treatment Volume × 0.017 = mL injected; two-RN verification signatures.
  • Phase completion documented. COLLECT → BUFFY → UVA (to 00:00) → PAUSE → ELUTRIATE → EMPTY BOWL → RINSE.
  • UV precaution education delivered. Patient teach-back documented.
  • Procedure summary. Blood processed, ECV %TBV, heparin total, fluid balance.
  • Adverse events.
  • Long-term tracking. Clinical response (skin score for cGVHD, FEV1 for CLAD/BOS, etc.), DVT/PE surveillance for GVHD patients, infections, catheter health.
