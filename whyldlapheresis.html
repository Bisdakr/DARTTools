Why LDL Apheresis?
When statins, PCSK9 inhibitors, and lifestyle still aren't enough: a deep but readable guide to the mechanical removal of LDL and Lp(a) — and why we hold the ACE-i for 24 hours.

TL;DR — The 60-second version
LDL apheresis is a mechanical solution to a genetic problem. Patients with familial hypercholesterolemia (FH) — homozygous or heterozygous with established cardiovascular disease and stubbornly high LDL despite maximal statin/PCSK9-inhibitor therapy — can have their LDL cleared from circulation by passing plasma through a column that selectively binds apoB-containing lipoproteins. The Kaneka Liposorber LA-15 uses dextran-sulfate cellulose columns to clear ~60–70% of LDL and Lp(a) in a single session lasting 2–4 hours. Performed every 1–2 weeks lifelong, it cuts MACE substantially in this otherwise-untreatable cohort.

01
How LDL Apheresis Works
Dual-circuit plasma separation + selective adsorption

The Liposorber LA-15 is an integrated apheresis system. Blood is drawn from the patient through a venous catheter or AV fistula and passed through a plasma separator (similar in principle to a hemodialysis membrane but with larger pores, allowing plasma to filter through while retaining cells). The cellular component returns to the patient via the return line, unmodified.

The separated plasma flows through one of two parallel dextran-sulfate cellulose adsorption columns. Dextran sulfate is a polyanion that binds positively charged apoB-containing lipoproteins (LDL, VLDL, Lp(a)) via electrostatic interaction. The "cleaned" plasma exits the column and is returned to the patient through the venous return line. The two columns alternate — while one is adsorbing, the other regenerates with a hypertonic saline elution buffer that strips bound LDL off the column for reuse.

🔑
What gets removed (and what doesn't)Removed: LDL-C (~70%), Lp(a) (~60–70%), VLDL (~50%), apoB-containing remnants. Not removed (preserved): HDL, immunoglobulins (mostly), albumin, clotting factors (mostly preserved). The selectivity is part of the safety story — you're not depleting the patient of plasma proteins generally, just lipid carriers.

The Liposorber requires heparin anticoagulation (not citrate, like centrifugal apheresis). The typical heparin order is 25 U/kg load + 25 U/kg/hr continuous infusion, titrated to ACT during the procedure. The DART LDL calculator computes these doses based on weight.

Despite > 60 years of dyslipidemia pharmacology, a meaningful subset of patients remain hypercholesterolemic on maximal therapy: statin + PCSK9 inhibitor + bempedoic acid + ezetimibe + lifestyle. For these patients, LDL apheresis adds a mechanical 60–70% session-acute reduction that metabolic effort cannot deliver.

Several LDL apheresis systems exist worldwide. The US-approved Kaneka Liposorber LA-15 uses dextran sulfate cellulose beads. Other systems use:

04
The ACE-i Problem
Why we hold lisinopril for 24 hours
⚠
A potentially fatal drug interactionDextran-sulfate and polyacrylate columns generate bradykinin from kininogen during plasma adsorption. In a patient on an ACE inhibitor (which blocks bradykinin degradation), bradykinin accumulates and causes severe hypotension, flushing, dyspnea, anaphylactoid reactions — sometimes fatal. The DART LDL calculator flags this with a red banner whenever ACE-i status is positive.

The institutional rule: hold ACE inhibitors at least 24 hours before LDL apheresis. Many centers prefer to switch the patient to an angiotensin-receptor blocker (ARB) entirely, which doesn't have the bradykinin-related interaction. The conversion: lisinopril → losartan (or equivalent). Patients should know to alert any new provider that this restriction exists.

Other systems (immunoadsorption, HELP) don't generate bradykinin as significantly, but the conservative practice is to hold ACE-i for any LDL apheresis modality.

05
Schedule & Goals
Why every 1–2 weeks, and what to target

After a single LDL apheresis session, LDL drops ~70%. Within hours, hepatic LDL re-synthesis begins, and within 1 week, LDL recovers to ~80% of pre-procedure values. By 2 weeks, it's nearly back to baseline. This is why scheduling is critical:

ScheduleTime-averaged LDL reductionTypical use
Weekly~46%HoFH; severe HeFH with active disease
Biweekly~35%HeFH + CAD; standard practice for most centers
Every 3 weeks~25%Rare; some Lp(a)-elevated patients on adjunct PCSK9i

Modern goals: get time-averaged LDL below 70 mg/dL (for secondary prevention) or below 55 mg/dL (for highest-risk recurrent events). These often require combining LDL apheresis with PCSK9 inhibitors and other oral agents.

06
Lp(a) — The Residual Risk Player
When statin + PCSK9i still aren't enough

Lipoprotein(a) is a genetically determined LDL-like particle with an additional apo(a) protein attached. Elevated Lp(a) (> 60 mg/dL or > 125 nmol/L by some assays) carries independent cardiovascular risk that statins don't address. PCSK9 inhibitors lower Lp(a) modestly (~20%). LDL apheresis remains one of the most effective ways to reduce Lp(a) acutely (~60–70% reduction per session).

🧪
Pipeline: Lp(a)-specific drugsPelacarsen (an antisense oligonucleotide targeting Lp(a)) and olpasiran (siRNA against Lp(a)) are in phase 3 trials. If these drugs demonstrate hard cardiovascular endpoint benefit (HORIZON, OCEAN(a)-Outcomes trials), the role of LDL apheresis for Lp(a)-elevated patients may shift. Until then, apheresis remains the most effective Lp(a) intervention available.
07
The FSGS Indication
A Kaneka FDA expanded use that surprises people

The Liposorber LA-15 also has FDA approval for recurrent focal segmental glomerulosclerosis (FSGS) after kidney transplant. The mechanism is hypothesized to be removal of a circulating permeability factor that causes recurrent disease in the new kidney. The exact molecule remains debated (suPAR is a candidate but not confirmed; cardiotrophin-like cytokine factor 1 [CLCF-1] is another).

The institutional protocol is typically a 12-session course over 9 weeks, then taper based on proteinuria response. Pediatric patients with recurrent FSGS post-transplant are common candidates. The technical procedure is identical to the LDL indication — the same columns, same parameters — only the indication and patient population differ.

08
2024–2025 Developments
Pharmacotherapy vs apheresis
💊
Inclisiran's growing roleInclisiran (an siRNA against PCSK9, dosed every 6 months) has expanded HoFH and HeFH treatment options. Some patients on stable inclisiran may be able to reduce apheresis frequency. Real-world data on combined apheresis + inclisiran is now accumulating.
🎯
Pelacarsen Phase 3 readoutsThe HORIZON trial (pelacarsen vs placebo in patients with Lp(a) ≥ 70 mg/dL and CVD) is reading out in 2024–2025. If positive, this could reshape the Lp(a)-elevated apheresis population.
🧬
Evinacumab in HoFHEvinacumab (anti-ANGPTL3 mAb) lowers LDL via a non-LDL-receptor pathway. Particularly relevant in HoFH where LDL-receptor activity is minimal. May further reduce apheresis frequency in select patients.
📊
Outcomes data continuesLong-term cohort studies (Japanese, German, US Liposorber registries) continue to demonstrate cardiovascular event reduction with chronic LDL apheresis in FH populations, both alone and combined with novel pharmacotherapies.
