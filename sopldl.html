LDL Apheresis SOP
A USA-aligned standard for performing chronic LDL apheresis using the Kaneka Liposorber LA-15 dextran-sulfate cellulose column system. Aligned with FDA-approved indications, AHA 2023 Scientific Statement on Lipoprotein Apheresis, ESC 2023 HoFH guidelines (Cuchel et al., Eur Heart J), and current device labeling.

Standard of Practice — Overview
LDL apheresis selectively removes apolipoprotein B-containing lipoproteins (LDL, VLDL, Lp(a)) from plasma using dextran-sulfate cellulose adsorption columns. This SOP describes the procedure using the FDA-approved Kaneka Liposorber LA-15 system. Indications include severe familial hypercholesterolemia (homozygous and heterozygous with CAD), elevated Lp(a) with established cardiovascular disease, and recurrent focal segmental glomerulosclerosis (FSGS) post-renal transplant. The most important safety verification is the ACE inhibitor hold ≥ 24 hours: dextran-sulfate columns generate bradykinin from kininogen, and concurrent ACE inhibition causes severe anaphylactoid reactions.

01
Purpose & Scope

Purpose: To define the standardized procedure for chronic LDL apheresis using the Kaneka Liposorber LA-15 system in patients with FDA-approved indications. The procedure typically continues lifelong on a 1–2 week schedule and requires durable vascular access strategy.

Scope: Adult and pediatric patients (typically ≥ 5 years for HoFH) with confirmed FDA-approved indication. Applies to apheresis registered nurses, apheresis physicians, cardiology / lipidology consultants, and pharmacy.

FDA-approved indications recognized: Homozygous Familial Hypercholesterolemia (HoFH); Heterozygous FH with documented CAD/PAD and LDL ≥ 100 mg/dL despite maximal therapy; Heterozygous FH with LDL ≥ 300 mg/dL without CAD; FH + CAD + Lp(a) ≥ 60 mg/dL with LDL ≥ 100 mg/dL despite maximal therapy; Recurrent FSGS post-renal transplant (HDE / expanded use).

02
ACE Inhibitor Verification — The Critical Safety Check
⚠
CRITICAL DRUG INTERACTIONDextran-sulfate and polyacrylate columns generate bradykinin from plasma kininogen during adsorption. In patients on ACE inhibitors (which block bradykinin degradation), bradykinin accumulates causing severe hypotension, facial flushing, dyspnea, anaphylactoid reactions. Fatal cases reported.
  1. Document ACE inhibitor status pre-procedure. Verify with patient (verbal) and electronic medical record. Specific agents to verify: lisinopril, enalapril, ramipril, captopril, benazepril, fosinopril, moexipril, perindopril, quinapril, trandolapril, sacubitril/valsartan (ARNI).
  2. Verify ≥ 24-hour hold. Confirm specific time of last dose. If hold < 24 hours, do not proceed; contact ordering physician.
  3. For chronic LDL apheresis patients, convert to ARB. Coordinate with cardiology / primary care for permanent conversion from ACE-i to ARB (losartan, valsartan, irbesartan, telmisartan) to avoid recurring hold-window issues.
  4. Aliskiren and sacubitril-valsartan have theoretical risk. Confirm with apheresis physician before proceeding; many institutions hold these similarly.
  5. Document the verification. Date and time of last ACE-i dose (or "confirmed never on ACE-i"); signature.
03
Roles & Responsibilities

Lipidology / Cardiology Physician

Confirms indication; coordinates with concurrent pharmacotherapy (statin, PCSK9i, ezetimibe, bempedoic acid, inclisiran); monitors LDL/Lp(a) trends; reviews schedule.

Apheresis Physician

Reviews ACE-i status pre-procedure; specifies plasma volume to process; available for emergent consultation.

Apheresis Primary RN

Performs verification including critical ACE-i hold check; operates the Liposorber system; monitors patient hemodynamics; documents.

Pharmacy

Supplies heparin (10,000 U vial for procedure); reviews drug interactions; coordinates ARB substitution if chronic patient.

Apheresis Medical Director

Maintains long-term institutional protocol; tracks chronic patient outcomes; coordinates with FH program if available.

04
Pre-Procedure Verification
  1. Patient identification — two identifiers.
  2. Informed consent — Standing consent for chronic patients per institutional renewal schedule; re-confirm understanding each session.
  3. ACE inhibitor verification — see Section 02. CRITICAL CHECK.
  4. Pre-procedure labs. Lipid panel (LDL, HDL, triglycerides, Lp(a) periodically), CBC with platelets (track HIT surveillance), comprehensive metabolic panel, ionized calcium (less concerning with heparin than citrate but still useful), Mg, baseline aPTT or ACT for heparin titration.
  5. Pre-procedure weight. Used to dose heparin (25 U/kg load + 25 U/kg/hr).
  6. Baseline vital signs.
  7. Vascular access verification. Two peripheral large-bore IVs (16–17 g) most common; AV fistula or tunneled apheresis catheter for difficult access; verify patency and condition.
  8. Vein preservation reminder for young chronic patients. Avoid PICC lines and BP cuffs on potential future AV fistula side.
  9. Concurrent lipid-lowering therapy verified active. Statin, PCSK9 inhibitor, ezetimibe, bempedoic acid, inclisiran — all should be continued.
  10. FSGS patients — Verify pediatric / transplant team coordination; confirm immunosuppression status.
05
System Setup & Priming

The Kaneka Liposorber LA-15 uses a dual-column system: while one column is adsorbing, the other regenerates with hypertonic saline elution. The plasma separator (analogous to a hemofilter) separates plasma from cells; plasma then passes through the adsorbent column and returns to mix with cells in the return line.

  1. Power on and self-test. Confirm Liposorber LA-15 procedure selected.
  2. Install plasma separator and adsorbent column set. Per manufacturer Instructions for Use. Verify proper orientation and tubing routing.
  3. Hang solutions. Saline 0.9% (typically 4–6 L total — initial prime + line management); Heparin 10,000 U in 500 mL NS (20 U/mL drip); Hypertonic saline regeneration solution per system.
  4. Program patient parameters. Weight, prescribed plasma volume to process (typical 3,000–4,000 mL adult), heparin bolus dose (25 U/kg = e.g., 1,750 U for 70 kg).
  5. Run automated prime & functional test. The system tests both columns sequentially; address any pressure or sensor issues.
  6. Stage saline for line flush at end of procedure.
  7. Verify ACT or aPTT plan for intraprocedure heparin monitoring. Target ACT 200–250 sec or aPTT 1.5–2.0× control. Institutional protocol governs interval.
06
Initiation
  1. Final pre-connection checks — ACE-i status reconfirmed.
  2. Sterile access connection. Don sterile gloves and mask. Establish both peripheral IVs (inlet + return) or central catheter access.
  3. Heparin bolus. Administer the calculated bolus (25 U/kg) through the appropriate access per institutional protocol — typically through the inlet line as the blood circuit is established.
  4. Initiate blood flow. Begin at low inlet flow (50–80 mL/min) and advance per machine prompt to target inlet flow (typically 80–120 mL/min).
  5. Begin heparin continuous infusion. 25 U/kg/hr maintenance drip; verify rate matches calculation.
  6. Monitor patient for first 5–10 minutes for ACE-i-type reaction even if hold confirmed — flushing, hypotension, dyspnea. Stop procedure immediately if any reaction.

08
Termination & Rinseback
  1. Verify prescribed plasma volume processed.
  2. Rinseback per machine prompt. Standard saline rinseback returns the circuit blood to the patient.
  3. Discontinue heparin infusion.
  4. Disconnect. Apply prolonged pressure to access sites (heparin patient — bleeding risk). Standard 10 minutes pressure for peripheral; longer for central catheter.
  5. Final post-procedure vital signs.
  6. Reassess for delayed bradykinin / allergic reaction during observation period.
09
Post-Procedure Management
  • Observation — Minimum 30 minutes bedside; monitor for delayed bradykinin reactions, late hypotension.
  • Post-procedure labs — Repeat lipid panel if first session (verify acute LDL reduction), CBC with platelets (HIT surveillance), aPTT if heparin reversal needed.
  • ACE-i restart timing — If patient was held but not converted to ARB, ACE-i may restart per physician timing (typically ≥ 24 hr post-procedure). Document instruction to patient.
  • Patient education — Bleeding precautions for 6–12 hours; sit/stand cautiously; report any persistent hypotension, flushing, dyspnea, severe headache, unexpected bleeding.
  • Schedule next session — Every 1–2 weeks per chronic prescription. Verify replacement column and supply availability.
  • Long-term monitoring — Lipid panel every 4–6 weeks initially, then less frequently; ferritin and CBC every 6 months (chronic blood loss); fat-soluble vitamin levels annually; bone density per cardiology / endocrinology recommendation.
