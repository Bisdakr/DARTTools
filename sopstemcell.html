HPC-A Standards of Practice
A USA-aligned standard for performing peripheral blood hematopoietic progenitor cell (HPC-A) collection using a continuous-flow centrifugal apheresis system (Spectra Optia CMNC). Aligned with FACT-JACIE International Standards, AABB Cellular Therapy Standards, and current device-operator references.

Standard of Practice — Overview
Peripheral blood Hematopoietic Progenitor Cell apheresis (HPC-A, also CMNC collection) collects mobilized CD34+ stem cells from a donor's bloodstream after mobilization with G-CSF, plerixafor, or both. The product is used for autologous or allogeneic stem cell transplantation, or for gene therapy manufacturing (SCD, β-thalassemia). This SOP describes the procedure using the Spectra Optia CMNC program. All collections are performed under FACT-JACIE International Standards for cellular therapy product collection. For sickle cell disease donors, G-CSF is contraindicated; plerixafor monotherapy is the only acceptable mobilization agent.

01
Purpose & Scope

Purpose: To define the standardized procedure for peripheral blood hematopoietic progenitor cell collection in adult and pediatric patients/donors for transplantation or cellular therapy manufacturing.

Scope: Applies to autologous patients, related and unrelated allogeneic donors, and patients undergoing CD34 mobilization for gene therapy. Performed under FACT-JACIE accreditation framework. Requires defined donor/patient eligibility per AABB Cellular Therapy Standards.

Indications: Autologous transplant in multiple myeloma, lymphoma, AML, neuroblastoma; allogeneic transplant in matched related/unrelated donor; gene therapy CD34 collection for sickle cell disease (lentiviral gene therapy), transfusion-dependent β-thalassemia, severe combined immunodeficiency, and selected metabolic diseases.

⚠
G-CSF Contraindicated in SCDPatients with sickle cell disease must NOT receive G-CSF (filgrastim). G-CSF can precipitate severe vaso-occlusive crisis, acute chest syndrome, and death in SCD. The only acceptable mobilization in SCD is plerixafor 0.24 mg/kg SQ, typically 6 hours before collection. Verify SCD status on every patient before any G-CSF order.
02
Roles & Responsibilities

Transplant Physician / Cellular Therapy Physician

Determines indication, prescribes mobilization, defines CD34 target dose; reviews donor/patient eligibility per FACT-JACIE; signs off on collection authorization.

Apheresis Physician

Reviews pre-procedure labs and peripheral CD34; specifies collection parameters; available for emergent consultation; reviews product release.

Apheresis Primary RN

Performs verification of donor/patient eligibility; operates machine; monitors and documents; handles product per chain-of-custody requirements.

Cellular Therapy Lab / Processing Facility

Receives the product per chain-of-custody; performs CD34 enumeration, cell count, viability; cryopreservation or fresh release; documentation per FACT-JACIE.

Pharmacy

Prepares ACD-A, prophylactic calcium, mobilization agents (G-CSF, plerixafor); verifies SCD contraindications before any G-CSF dispensing.

Donor Advocate (allogeneic only)

Independent role to ensure donor safety and autonomy; verifies informed consent; available for donor questions through procedure.

03
Mobilization Regimen Verification
  1. Confirm mobilization plan in order set. Identify the specific agent(s) and dose(s) for this patient: G-CSF dose and number of days, plerixafor dose and timing, chemotherapy mobilization if applicable.
  2. Verify SCD status if G-CSF is ordered. Critical safety check. If patient has SCD, G-CSF must NOT be administered. Contact ordering physician to convert to plerixafor monotherapy.
  3. Pre-collection peripheral CD34. Obtain peripheral blood CD34 count the morning of collection. Adequacy criteria vary by institution but typical thresholds: ≥ 10 cells/µL acceptable; ≥ 20 cells/µL favorable; < 10 cells/µL may require additional plerixafor or reschedule.
  4. Pre-collection labs. CBC with differential and platelet count; comprehensive metabolic panel; ionized calcium. Confirm WBC sufficient for collection (typically > 5 × 10⁹/L for adult autologous; donor-specific thresholds for allogeneic).
  5. Donor/patient symptom check. Inquire about bone pain (G-CSF), headache, fatigue, GI symptoms; assess fitness to proceed; document any reactions to mobilization.
04
Pre-Procedure Verification
  1. Patient/donor identification — two identifiers per policy. Confirm against all documentation including the cellular therapy product label.
  2. Informed consent verification. Apheresis collection consent + cellular therapy product use consent (for the recipient indication). For allogeneic donors, additional donor-specific consent regarding risks and donor advocacy contact.
  3. FACT-JACIE eligibility documentation. Verify required donor screening (infectious disease testing, ABO/Rh, HLA, weight, vitals) is complete and current per institutional standards. The product cannot be released for use without this documentation.
  4. Order verification. Read back: TBV multiplier (typically 2.0–3.0), AC ratio (12:1 default; 8:1 for SCD/TDT), CD34 target dose, expected product volume, processing plan, anticipated cryopreservation.
  5. Vascular access verification. Central catheter (≥ 10 Fr typical for adult autologous) often required for adequate flow rates. Peripheral acceptable for healthy related donors with good antecubital vein quality. Verify patency.
  6. Baseline vital signs and weight. Used for TBV calculation and AIR limit.
  7. Calcium replacement plan. Prophylactic IV calcium gluconate drip is common for HPC-A given the citrate load over a 2–3 TBV processing. Standing order verified.
  8. Product label preparation. ISBT 128 ICCBBA-compliant labels with patient ID, donor ID, date, product code prepared in advance per institutional process.
05
Machine Setup, Prime & Functional Test
  1. Power on and self-test. Select CMNC procedure. Verify the disposable set is CMNC-rated (different set from TPE/RBCX).
  2. Install the CMNC disposable set. Follow on-screen prompts; verify cassette orientation, pump segments, collection chamber, plasma-divert bag.
  3. Hang solutions. ACD-A 1000 mL × 1–2 (yellow spike, expect 600–800 mL used for adult 2 TBV); NS 4 L (white spike).
  4. Program patient/donor parameters. Sex, height, weight, baseline Hct. Verify TBV calculation. Enter TBV multiplier (e.g., 2.5), AC ratio (12:1 default), Collection Preference setting initial.
  5. Set collection preference appropriately for product type. Standard autologous / allogeneic: lighter end (less RBC contamination, cleaner MNC product). SCD/TDT gene therapy: darker end (denser cell collection per manufacturer requirement). Verify with cellular therapy lab.
  6. Verify AIR cap. Machine AIR limit 1.2 mL/min/L TBV is enforced automatically. Tighter AC ratios reduce maximum allowable inlet flow proportionally.
  7. Run prime cycle to completion. Address any prime test failures.
  8. Stage product collection bag. Verify ISBT 128 label affixed to the collection bag before any product enters.
06
Initiation
  1. Pre-connection: final ID checks. Verify patient/donor, product label, mobilization agent given, baseline vitals.
  2. Sterile access. Don sterile gloves and mask. Prep access. Connect inlet and return lines.
  3. Slow-start. Begin inlet 25 mL/min, return 10 mL/min. Observe for citrate symptoms, vasovagal response.
  4. Advance to steady-state flow. Adult: 50–80 mL/min typical, constrained by AIR ceiling. Pediatric/small adult: weight-based, typically 1.5–2.5 mL/kg/min initial.
  5. Begin collection phase per machine prompt. Watch for "Collecting MNC" indicator. Verify Collection Preference is appropriate setting.
  6. Verify TBV processed counter and product accumulation. Confirm processing progress matches plan.

💡
AIR Cap and Throughput OptimizationIf AIR is consistently at the 1.2 ceiling and inlet flow is throttled below target, two options: (1) loosen AC ratio (e.g., 12:1 → 13:1) to permit higher inlet flow at lower citrate dose-rate per liter; or (2) accept lower TBV processed and discuss extended collection. Tightening AC ratio (e.g., 12:1 → 8:1) reduces inlet flow further but is necessary for SCD/TDT gene therapy collections requiring high cell density.
08
Product Handling & Chain of Custody
  1. End-of-collection bag seal. Once TBV target processed and machine prompts to end collection, seal the product bag per institutional protocol; do not break sterility.
  2. Verify ISBT 128 label. Confirm patient/donor ID, donor type code, expiration, product code on the bag matches the order and cellular therapy lab expectations.
  3. Sample for processing facility. Per institutional protocol, withdraw aliquot(s) for cell count, viability, CD34 enumeration, and any required microbiology / mycoplasma testing.
  4. Product temperature management. Maintain at 2–24°C per institutional standard; do not warm above body temperature unless required by storage protocol; do not freeze the product itself prior to controlled cryopreservation.
  5. Chain-of-custody documentation. Two-person verification at transfer: from apheresis bedside to courier, courier to cellular therapy lab. Both signatures on the chain-of-custody form. Time logged.
  6. Cellular therapy lab notification. Confirm receipt; lab confirms accession and begins processing per their SOP.
09
Post-Procedure
  • Observation — Minimum 30 minutes bedside; longer if pediatric or first session.
  • Post-procedure labs — CBC with platelets (expect drop), ionized calcium, magnesium, citrate-related metabolic check if symptomatic.
  • Post-procedure CD34 result follow-up — From cellular therapy lab; verify adequacy for the indication.
  • Schedule additional session(s) if needed — Per CD34 yield. Multi-day collections are common for myeloma, allogeneic, and SCD/TDT gene therapy.
  • Patient education — Mild fatigue and bone pain are expected; report fever, severe LUQ pain (G-CSF splenic concern), persistent headache, unusual bleeding/bruising.
  • Donor follow-up — For allogeneic donors, schedule 4-week and 1-year follow-up per FACT-JACIE / WMDA standards; document in donor health registry.

Documentation & Quality Indicators
  • Identifiers and FACT-JACIE eligibility — Complete donor screening package, ISBT 128 product labels, ABO/Rh.
  • Mobilization detail — Agent(s), doses, dates, response.
  • Pre-collection peripheral CD34 — Documented numeric value with collection date.
  • Procedure detail — TBV processed, AC ratio, inlet flow trends, AIR, Collection Preference setting and changes, citrate symptoms screens, vital signs.
  • Product detail — Final volume, ISBT 128 label, samples sent, chain-of-custody.
  • CD34 yield — From cellular therapy lab; documented at procedure record close.
  • Adverse events — Time, type, intervention, response.
  • Communication / handoff — SBAR to receiving unit.
