WBC Depletion SOP
A USA-aligned standard for performing therapeutic white blood cell depletion (leukapheresis) for hyperleukocytic acute leukemia using a continuous-flow centrifugal apheresis system (Spectra Optia CMNC). Aligned with ASFA 2023 Special Issue, ASH guidance on hyperleukocytosis management, and European LeukemiaNet APL recommendations.

Standard of Practice — Overview
Therapeutic leukapheresis is performed emergently in hyperleukocytic acute leukemia with clinical or impending leukostasis (WBC typically > 100 × 10⁹/L with pulmonary, cerebral, or other end-organ compromise). The procedure provides bridging cytoreduction while definitive chemotherapy is initiated. The single most important safety check is exclusion of Acute Promyelocytic Leukemia (APL, AML-M3) — leukapheresis is contraindicated in APL due to worsening of severe coagulopathy. Tumor lysis syndrome (TLS) prophylaxis must be active before initiating.

01
Purpose & Scope

Purpose: To define the standardized procedure for emergent therapeutic white blood cell depletion (leukapheresis) in patients with hyperleukocytic acute leukemia and clinical or impending leukostasis. Leukapheresis is bridging therapy — not definitive treatment — and must be combined with concurrent or imminent cytoreductive chemotherapy.

Scope: Adult and pediatric patients with confirmed acute leukemia, hyperleukocytosis (WBC typically > 100 × 10⁹/L), with symptomatic leukostasis or high risk thereof, and absence of APL. Applies to all licensed apheresis registered nurses, apheresis physicians, hematology / oncology staff, blood bank staff, and pharmacy.

Indications recognized: Symptomatic hyperleukocytosis in AML (Category II); symptomatic hyperleukocytosis in ALL (Category II); CML blast crisis with symptomatic hyperleukocytosis (Category III); priapism with hyperleukocytosis. Per ASFA 2023.

02
APL Exclusion — The Critical Pre-Procedure Verification
⚠
ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATIONAcute promyelocytic leukemia (APL, AML-M3) is an absolute contraindication to therapeutic leukapheresis. APL is characterized by severe coagulopathy (DIC and hyperfibrinolysis). Leukapheresis worsens this profoundly and frequently triggers fatal intracranial hemorrhage. Patients with APL receive ATRA (all-trans retinoic acid) immediately, often before genetic confirmation; this resolves the coagulopathy and reduces hyperleukocytosis without need for apheresis.
  1. Confirm leukemia subtype before authorizing leukapheresis. Review flow cytometry, blood smear, and rapid molecular testing (PML-RARA RT-PCR) results. Hematology consultation mandatory before procedure.
  2. Index of suspicion for APL. AML with DIC at presentation, characteristic morphology (hypergranular or microgranular promyelocytes), low PT/PTT abnormalities, bilineal involvement. Any concern for APL → STOP, contact hematology, start ATRA.
  3. Document subtype verification. Pathology report referenced in procedure record. Two-clinician verification (apheresis physician + hematologist) for any borderline case.
  4. If APL confirmed/suspected: Procedure does not proceed. Start ATRA 45 mg/m²/day (split BID). Aggressive coagulopathy treatment: FFP, cryoprecipitate, platelets to maintain plt > 30k, fibrinogen > 150 mg/dL.
03
Roles & Responsibilities

Apheresis Physician

Reviews indication and APL exclusion; specifies TBV multiplier and AC strategy; available for emergent consultation.

Hematology / Oncology Service

Confirms subtype, coordinates definitive chemotherapy timing, manages TLS / DIC prophylaxis, monitors clinical leukostasis response.

Apheresis Primary RN

Performs all pre-procedure verifications including APL exclusion documentation; operates machine; monitors and documents.

Blood Bank

Prepares blood products for DIC/bleeding (platelets, cryoprecipitate, FFP); type and screen current; coordinates emergency release if needed.

Pharmacy

Supplies TLS prophylaxis (allopurinol or rasburicase), ACD-A, calcium replacement; coordinates with hematology for chemotherapy preparation.

04
Pre-Procedure Verification
  1. Two-identifier patient ID.
  2. Informed consent. Apheresis consent + transfusion consent (likely needed for blood prime or bleeding management).
  3. APL exclusion verified and documented. Pathology / flow cytometry report attached or referenced.
  4. Pre-procedure labs. CBC with differential, peripheral blast count, comprehensive metabolic panel, ionized calcium, magnesium, phosphorus, uric acid, LDH, fibrinogen, PT/PTT, D-dimer, type and screen, antibody screen.
  5. TLS prophylaxis confirmed active. IV fluids ≥ 2–3 L/m²/day, allopurinol or rasburicase (preferred for very high WBC), K and phosphate restriction, q4–6 hr TLS labs.
  6. Coagulation profile review. Platelets, fibrinogen, PT/PTT, D-dimer documented. Replacement plan if needed: plt < 30k → transfuse; fibrinogen < 100 → cryoprecipitate.
  7. Vascular access verification. Central catheter standard given coagulopathy and flow requirements.
  8. Baseline vital signs.
  9. Calcium replacement plan and prophylactic calcium drip.
  10. Hematology / oncology coordination. Confirm chemotherapy timing — leukapheresis bridges to chemo; both should be within hours of each other for optimal outcome.

06
Machine Setup, Prime & Test
  1. Power on; select CMNC procedure (same set as HPC-A collection). Verify setting reflects therapeutic depletion (TBV-based, not target CD34).
  2. Install CMNC disposable set.
  3. Hang solutions. ACD-A, NS, return-line warmer (verify operating temperature).
  4. Program patient parameters. Sex, height, weight, baseline WBC, Hct. Enter target TBV multiplier (typically 1.5–2.0). AC ratio 12:1 default; institutional discretion if tighter ratio needed for clumping.
  5. Prime & functional test cycle.
  6. Verify rinseback setting per institutional protocol; typically ON with saline prime in WBC depletion since the collection bag retains leukocytes that are discarded.
  7. Verify collection preference setting — moderate to slightly darker for therapeutic WBC depletion (maximize WBC removal per pass).
07
Initiation
  1. Final pre-connection checks.
  2. Sterile access connection.
  3. Slow-start. Inlet 25 mL/min, return 10 mL/min for first 5 min; observe for citrate symptoms, vasovagal response, bleeding signs at access.
  4. Advance to target flow. Adult: 50–80 mL/min steady-state. Constrained by AIR ceiling and patient tolerance.
  5. Document timestamp of WBC counter zero.

09
Termination
  1. Verify TBV processed and WBC reduction. Document final TBV, total ACD, total NS, expected WBC reduction.
  2. Rinseback per machine prompt if saline prime; standard institutional protocol.
  3. Disconnect patient with extra care given coagulopathy — extended pressure on access sites; specialized dressing.
  4. Post-procedure WBC stat. Verify magnitude of reduction; communicate to oncology.
  5. Coordinate chemotherapy initiation if not already started.
10
Post-Procedure
  • Observation — Minimum 60 minutes bedside; longer if hemodynamic, neurologic, or respiratory concerns.
  • Post-procedure labs — CBC with differential, comprehensive metabolic panel, TLS labs (K, phos, uric acid, Ca), fibrinogen, PT/PTT, D-dimer, type and screen.
  • Coordinate with oncology for next leukapheresis if WBC rebounds and chemotherapy is delayed; daily sessions may be needed.
  • Continuous coagulopathy and bleeding surveillance.
  • Definitive chemotherapy — confirm scheduled and initiated; the procedure is bridging, not curative.
  • ICU-level monitoring typically required.

Documentation & Quality Indicators
  • APL exclusion documented. Pathology report referenced; two-clinician verification on borderline cases.
  • TLS prophylaxis documented. Hydration, allopurinol/rasburicase, K/phos baseline labs.
  • Pre-procedure coagulation profile. Platelets, fibrinogen, PT/PTT, D-dimer.
  • Procedure detail. TBV processed, AC ratio, inlet flow trends, vital signs.
  • WBC reduction. Pre and post WBC documented numerically.
  • Communication. Hematology / oncology coordination of chemotherapy timing.
  • Adverse events. Time, type, intervention, response.
